About Me

I am a PhD student at Technical University of Darmstadt in the Department of Finance. My research focuses on applying machine learning techniques to asset pricing and portfolio optimization. With over a decade of experience in portfolio management, I bring a unique practical perspective to my academic work.

My research is particularly informed by my professional experience in asset allocation and portfolio construction, allowing me to bridge theoretical frameworks with real-world applications. I am currently working with Prof. Dr. Dirk Schiereck on my thesis titled "Machine Learning in Asset Pricing."

My research interests include:

Machine Learning in Finance Asset Pricing Portfolio Optimization Factor Investing Portfolio Management Investment Strategy Risk Management

Current Research

My current research explores the application of machine learning techniques to enhance asset pricing models and portfolio optimization strategies. By leveraging advanced algorithms, I aim to identify non-linear patterns in financial data that traditional models may overlook, ultimately improving risk-adjusted returns.

My research is uniquely informed by my extensive professional experience in portfolio management. This practical background allows me to identify research questions with high real-world relevance and develop solutions that address actual challenges faced by industry practitioners.

I employ a combination of Python-based machine learning frameworks (e.g., TensorFlow, scikit-learn) and financial data platforms (e.g., Bloomberg, Refinitiv Eikon) to conduct my analyses. My work also involves developing proprietary indicators and fair value models to support discretionary investment decisions.

Key industry insights incorporated in my research:

  • Practical experience in managing multi-asset portfolios informs my approach to risk management and asset allocation.
  • Insights from developing tailor-made portfolio services for ultra-high-net-worth individuals guide my focus on client-centric solutions.
  • My approach integrates industry-relevant metrics, such as risk-adjusted performance and portfolio drawdowns, unlike purely theoretical models.

For more details about my research, please see my publications or download my CV.
